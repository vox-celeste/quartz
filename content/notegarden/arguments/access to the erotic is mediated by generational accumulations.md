People are assemblages of experiences that determine health and unhealth, including intergenerational trauma and displacement. These legacies can make themselves felt in all somatic work, including engagement with erotic feeling.
## references

> Each person we encounter is an accumulation of social, physical, emotional, mental, spiritual, financial, and environmental health. They also hold their ancestors' experiences. Difficulty being in our bodies (even with a desire to feel embodied) or accessing the erotic can come from physical or nonphysical causes. It can look like individual, collective, and/or ancestral trauma.

⤷ Jamee Pineda, "A Decolonizing Approach to the Erotic and Medicine" in _Trans Sex_, Lucie Fielding
